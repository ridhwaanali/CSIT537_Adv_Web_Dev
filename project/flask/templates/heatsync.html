On This Page You Will Learn About CPU Heat Syncs

A CPU Heat sync is the fan that sits on top of the CPU.

Its purpose is to exhaust the heat generated from the CPU when it is used

The way it is structured is there is a metal plate to sit on top of the CPU, (most likley Copper), and it condenses the heat through the metal plate out with a fan

Modern computers use air heat syncs and liquid heat syncs

Air heat syncs just use the metal plate and fan to exhaust heat whereas the liquid heat sync uses a liquid cooling to push out the heat quicker

The heat syncs usually are held with clamps located at the back of the motherboard and fans are connected on the "cpu fan header"

Note not all CPU fans function with all CPUs please check compatability
